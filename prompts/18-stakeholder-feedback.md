## Round 1 - 2 July 2026

### Joel

Good morning all.  I've just taken a look through the website again.  Here's my thinking, which of course is just my opinion!
I think for this recruitment phase we need it to be a lot simpler and more positive about "Route 1".  It doesn't really go into what we hope to achieve quickly and easily, which is to offer JLR a route to the opinions and preferences of the drivers involved, so that they can quickly make an offer that is acceptable to as many as possible.  It should mention that we wish to resolve this ahead of the new car launch so that we can be part of a positive outcome all round.  Basically, I'm looking at this as the JLR execs who will be reading it and - hopefully - realising that we are now organised, so the game has changed.  Even though the page is written for members, it's actually the shot across the bows that JLR will read first, so it needs to get the "golden bridge / burning bridge" options on there.
At the moment it is all about a thing we've not really discussed, which is all the technical and data side.  That may well be needed, but for now I think the main message is lost in that.   Can I propose that we hide most of it for now and just say that we will be collecting and using data to provide the best possible picture of the problems and needs of the members?  Then we can open up that side if and when required.  For now I'm thinking of two use cases:
1. Potential member, who's just a normal driver uninterested in and probably baffled by all the data and recalls and so on.  They just want a quick, positive message and a CTA to register with minimum data for us to move forward.  Perhaps the option to add the vehicle, but no expectation to do so at this stage.
2. JLR execs and lawyers.  They need to read it and think, "oh shit; we need to re-think this!".
Hopefully useful?

## Round 2 implementation interpretation

- Merge the duplicated homepage positioning into one launch headline: "I-PACE owners working
  together for fair outcomes".
- Present the launch site as UK-led. Non-UK owners may still register interest, but any JLR
  approach, legal representation, or market-specific outcome must be scoped separately.
- Trim launch-visible FAQ language so it leads with purpose, joining, privacy, scope, and legal
  status rather than evidence methodology. Full-mode data/methodology detail remains available.
- Reinforce that this is aligned advocacy, not an open forum, not a moany comment board, and
  not a mechanism for fragmented individual settlement demands. Individual owners may still
  pursue their own routes; the group cannot and should not claim otherwise.
